About the Workshop

Why Medical AI Fails
— and How to Fix It

Despite remarkable technical advances, evidence that AI tools create real value for patients, providers, or health systems remains scarce. BRIDGE exists to change that.

The Problem

The Gap Between
Performance and Value

Artificial intelligence is reshaping medical imaging and healthcare at a remarkable pace. Yet a troubling pattern has emerged: models that excel in research settings routinely fail to deliver meaningful benefit once deployed in clinical practice.

The accelerating pace of AI development is outpacing the development of corresponding evaluation and regulatory frameworks. This disconnect creates a widening gap that not only hinders effective integration of AI into healthcare but raises serious concerns about patient safety.

The field has invested heavily in building and benchmarking AI models, but far less in asking whether those models actually help. Evaluation without value measurement is insufficient. Regulation without deployment accountability is incomplete.

"If we cannot evaluate it, we cannot measure its value. If we cannot measure its value, we should not deploy it. And if we do not monitor it, we cannot sustain it."

What the Evidence Says
Nature Medicine, 2026

"Evidence that AI tools create value for patients, providers or health systems remains scarce." Evaluation has relied mostly on statistical metrics that measure computational capabilities but do not establish clinical impact.

Nature Medicine, 2026

"A system may perform very well in retrospective validation and still fail to improve care" if its outputs are poorly timed, difficult to interpret, or disruptive to clinical workflows.

Nature Medicine, 2025

Could AI models be quietly steering us towards expensive or unnecessary treatments? "Regulation and evaluation are intricately connected" — the principle "if we cannot evaluate it, we cannot regulate it" highlights this dependency.
