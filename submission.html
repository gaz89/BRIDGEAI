Call for Submissions

Submit Your Research

We invite original research, empirical studies, position papers, and negative results across all four stages of the BRIDGE pipeline — and the critical transitions between them.

Submit on OpenReview
Topics of Interest

Research Topics

Papers are welcome on topics related — but not limited — to the four stages of the BRIDGE pipeline.

Stage 01

Evaluate It

  • Robust evaluation methods for AI-enabled medical devices
  • Benchmarking tools, simulation platforms, and digital twins
  • Evaluation of generative AI, LLMs, and autonomous systems
  • Distribution shift, dataset bias, and generalization failures
  • Evaluation pitfalls, negative results, and flawed practices
  • Metrics beyond accuracy: calibration, uncertainty, fairness
  • Explainable AI outputs and regulatory expectations
Stage 02

Measure Its Clinical Value

  • Frameworks for defining and measuring clinical utility
  • The gap between model performance and clinical improvement
  • Studies showing AI adds — or fails to add — real-world value
  • Cost-effectiveness and health economic analyses of AI systems
  • Value misalignment: when AI optimizes for the wrong objective
  • Human factors, clinician behavior, and decision support
  • Clinical validation and end-user feedback in algorithm evaluation
Stage 03

Deploy It Responsibly

  • Why AI models fail after deployment: workflow, trust, drift
  • Case studies of research-to-clinic transitions
  • Sociotechnical and implementation science perspectives
  • Equity, fairness, and disparate impact in deployed AI systems
  • Cross-regional regulatory pathways: EU, US, Asia
  • Regulatory science frameworks for AI/ML medical devices
  • Early regulatory engagement for academics, startups, consortia
Stage 04

Monitor It

  • Post-market monitoring and long-term safety surveillance
  • Model drift detection and safe update strategies
  • Continual learning systems under regulatory constraints
  • Frameworks for real-world AI performance monitoring
  • Safety updates, version control, and lifecycle management
  • Adaptive regulatory frameworks for evolving AI systems
  • FDA's PCCP and pre-determined change control plans

Submission Format

All submissions must use the official Springer LNCS format and be fully anonymized for double-blind review.

  • Official Springer LNCS format (Word & LaTeX templates)
  • Up to 8 pages of main content
  • Up to 2 additional pages for references only
  • Double-blind review — submissions must be fully anonymized
  • Omit author names, affiliations, and self-identifying references
  • Submit via OpenReview

Accepted papers will be published in the MICCAI Workshops volume of Springer's Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series.

Evaluation Criteria

Criterion 01

Relevance

Alignment with AI evaluation, clinical value measurement, regulatory science, or deployment challenges in healthcare. Papers on all four pipeline stages are in scope.

Criterion 02

Clarity & Structure

Logical organization, clear writing, and accessibility to a multidisciplinary audience of researchers, clinicians, and regulatory scientists.

Criterion 03

Empirical Rigor

Robust measurement or validation of new or existing concepts (for empirical work). Position papers and negative results are also welcome and valued.
