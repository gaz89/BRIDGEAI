About the Workshop

Building Medical AI That Works — Safe, Effective, and Clinically Valuable

Medical AI has made remarkable technical progress, yet much of the field remains focused on developing state-of-the-art models, benchmarking them on static datasets, and optimizing technical metrics that may not fully capture clinical risk, clinical benefit, or real-world value. A model can perform well in a research setting and still fail to meaningfully support clinical decisions, improve patient outcomes, or integrate safely into healthcare workflows. In some cases, poorly translated AI systems may introduce new risks for patients, clinicians, and health systems.

BRIDGE focuses on closing the gap between AI innovation, evaluation, and regulation to enable safe and effective clinical translation. The workshop is grounded in a simple idea: medical AI should be developed with translation in mind from the very beginning. Before a model is built, we must ask what clinical problem it is intended to solve, what decision or action it will support, how it will benefit patients, and what value it will add for clinicians and healthcare systems.

This clinical end goal should guide every stage of AI development, from data collection and model design to validation, deployment, monitoring, and regulatory evaluation. At each stage, we need clear mechanisms to identify and measure risk, select metrics that reflect the clinical and safety context, and ensure that development decisions remain aligned with patient safety, responsible AI, and real-world clinical value.

What Makes BRIDGE Different

Regulation is not a checkpoint

While many MICCAI workshops focus on technical development, BRIDGE uniquely addresses evaluation pitfalls, clinical value evidence, deployment failures, and regulatory science as a unified challenge.

We bring together AI researchers, clinicians, regulatory scientists (including ex-FDA experts), health economists, and implementers to tackle all four pipeline stages.

The Framework

The BRIDGE Pipeline

Four stages. Each depends on the one before it. Skipping or shortcutting any stage is precisely why so many AI systems fail after deployment.

⚗️
Stage 01
Evaluate It
Rigorous technical evaluation — accuracy, robustness, generalizability, and failure modes — before any clinical claim can be made.
📊
Stage 02
Measure Its Clinical Value
Does the AI improve patient outcomes? Does it change clinical decisions for the better? Statistical performance alone does not answer this.
🏥
Stage 03
Deploy It Responsibly
Evidence-backed, workflow-integrated, equity-aware deployment with transparency about limitations and conditions of safe use.
📡
Stage 04
Monitor It
Sustained post-market vigilance: detecting drift, updating models safely, ensuring long-term safety and effectiveness.
⚖️
Regulation: The Governing Backbone

Regulation is not Stage 5 — it runs through all four stages. It defines rigor at evaluation, evidence thresholds for clinical value, conditions for deployment, and mandates for post-market monitoring.
